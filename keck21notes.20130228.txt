-----------
MCMC Models 
-----------

The models are based on a joint fit to the RVs and transit photometry.  The adopted stellar parameters are listed first (R,M,Z,Teff,L,Age,log(g),<rhostar>.  Followed by parameters for the transit model <rhostar>,gamma,T0,P,b,r/R*,K and if fit ecosw,esinw followed by derived parameters Mp, Rp, i, a/R*, a, Tdepth, <rho_p>, log(g)_p, Teq.  <rhostar> is constrained in the transit fit by the stellar parameterization so it is listed twice.

I've done 2 sets of models for each system.  A simplified model with circular orbits (except when eccentricity is clearly seen) and a second model allowing for fully eccentric models. 

Teq was computed with:

Teq = Ts sqrt(Rs/2a) [f (1-Ab)] ^ 0.25,

where Ts is the effective temperature of the star, Rs is the stellar radius, a is the planet semi-major axis, Ab is the Bond albedo and f is a proxy for day-night redistribution with 1=full redistribution and 2 for no redistribution.  I assumed that Ab is a random number from 0 to 0.5 and f is a random number from 1 to 2.
